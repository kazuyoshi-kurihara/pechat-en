• You can use common abbreviations such as "Mr." and "St." and Chappet will interpret them as "mister" and "street."
  • You can also use symbols such as "%" and "$" and Chappet will say "percent" and "dollar."
Try different ways to write words or punctuate sentences to change the intonation of Chappet's speech.
  • Use "," (commas) to punctuate long sentences for Chappet to speak naturally.
  • Use ”?” (question mark) to formulate a question.
    Use “!” (exclamation mark) to formulate an exclamation.
Chappet only speaks in English.
You can only use ASCII characters and symbols. Other characters are not supported, therefore, will not show on the screen.
